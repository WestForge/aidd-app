# Audience and Users

## AIDD Users

StormbaneAIDD serves the people and agents turning the Stormbane GDD into coherent design, tools, code, and delivery packages.

### Project Owner / Design Lead

Needs the foundation to preserve the intended product direction, identify drift, expose weak assumptions, and keep future work aligned with the GDD rather than whatever the current implementation happens to contain.

### Gameplay Programmers

Need concise system intent, module ownership, boundaries, data flow, replication assumptions, acceptance checks, and clear statements of what not to build yet.

### Tools and Editor Programmers

Need authoring workflow expectations for kit setup, generation, validation, data assets, diagnostics, deterministic outputs, designer editing, baking, and runtime handoff.

### Designers and Content Authors

Need stable language for regions, POIs, factions, vendors, quests, rewards, marks, classes, rooms, structures, source confidence, and world-state rules so content can be authored consistently.

### AI Coding / Design Agents

Need explicit design intent, constraints, file ownership expectations, naming expectations, module boundaries, source confidence, delivery horizon, and acceptance evidence so generated work is focused and reviewable.

### Reviewers and QA

Need measurable acceptance criteria, known risks, expected behaviours, declared reward categories, multiplayer assumptions, and traceability back to the foundation, GDD, or capability definition.

## Player Audience

Stormbane is aimed at players who enjoy skill-based multiplayer RPGs, grounded medieval dark fantasy, faction conflict, tactical combat, world consequence, and long-term legacy systems.

The strongest audience fit is players who:

- Prefer tactical timing, positioning, and preparation over pure gear score.
- Want faction wars, territory control, and world-state change to matter.
- Enjoy danger, attrition, recovery cost, and meaningful failure.
- Like social reputation, prestige, titles, and earned recognition.
- Value local economies, scarcity, repair, vendors, logistics, and planning.
- Accept that dangerous locations can offer rare rewards without turning into random gear fountains.
- Enjoy emergent quests and systemic consequences more than static mission lists.
- Accept slower, more deliberate play when the world gives clear signals and fair choices.
- Care about House identity, heraldry, legacy, and role in the world.

## Player Motivations

### Mastery

Players should feel that improvement comes from learning combat timing, attack commitment, spacing, stamina, class roles, enemy behaviour, and battlefield positioning.

### Legacy

Players should want their House, title, reputation, scars, mount history, victories, losses, and faction contribution to become part of their identity.

### Social and Faction Belonging

Players should care about who controls territory, which faction they support, how NPCs treat them, and whether services or quests are available because of past actions.

### Exploration and Discovery

Players should be pulled through the world by landmarks, danger signals, POI cadence, roads, rumours, weather, NPC hints, and visible consequences.

### Planning and Logistics

Players should make deliberate decisions about gear durability, consumables, vendors, travel route, item transfer points, mount use, recovery options, and risk depth.

## Accessibility of Experience

The game can be harsh without being unreadable. Player-facing systems should clearly communicate danger, travel direction, tactical openings, service access, recovery cost, and faction state. Hidden systems such as Marks may remain diegetic and indirect, but their symptoms should be learnable and diagnosable through world clues.

## Poor Fit / Anti-Audience

Stormbane is less suited to players who primarily want:

- Fast power levelling and constant stat inflation.
- Random loot explosions from enemies and containers.
- Frictionless global markets and instant logistics.
- Unrestricted modular housing or construction as a primary promise.
- Free instant healing, death, travel, and mount summoning.
- A world that resets quickly and avoids lasting consequence.
- Fully safe settlements everywhere.
- Opaque procedural generation that cannot be inspected or tuned by designers.

These are not defects to remove by default; they are intentional boundaries unless the GDD direction changes.
